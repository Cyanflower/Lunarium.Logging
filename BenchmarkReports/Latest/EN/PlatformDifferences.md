### IV. LoggerThroughput — Caller-Side Throughput

This is the benchmark with the largest cross-platform divergence.

#### Single Log() Call (ns)

| Platform | No Props | 1 Prop | 3 Props | 5 Props | ForContext |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| WindowsPC | 153 | 168 | 169 | 185 | 163 |
| LinuxLaptop | 188 | 199 | 190 | 201 | 185 |
| WindowsServer | 380 | 250 | 321 | 303 | 397 |
| LinuxServer | 576 | 433 | 539 | 539 | 692 |

**Key Difference 1 — WindowsPC is the fastest:**
The i7-7700's high clock speed (3.6 GHz) leads LinuxLaptop (2.2 GHz) by ~20% and the two EPYC machines by approximately 2–4×.

**Key Difference 2 — LinuxServer is dramatically slower and highly unstable:**
- No-props: 576 ±133 ns (CV **23%**); all other platforms have CV in the 3–10% range
- ForContext: 692 ±145 ns (CV 21%)
- Same CPU model as WindowsServer (EPYC 7542), yet **1.5–2× slower** on Linux with severe jitter

This is the most noteworthy cross-platform finding. The same CPU exhibits markedly different behavior between Windows and Linux VPS environments, pointing toward OS-level differences or differences in how .NET is implemented on different operating systems. The high CV also indicates that Linux VPS scheduling jitter has a particularly pronounced effect on this scenario. The specific mechanism is pending further analysis.
